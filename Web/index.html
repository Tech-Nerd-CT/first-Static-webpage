Premier League: History, Founding & Rules

  • Founded: 20 February 1992 by 22 First Division clubs seeking financial independence.
  • Goal: Maximize TV revenue and create a new elite competition.
  • Format: 20 teams, 38 matches per season, 3 points for a win.
  • Relegation: Bottom 3 clubs drop to EFL Championship.
  • Top 4: Qualify for UEFA Champions League.
  • VAR: Used for critical decision reviews.
  • The inaugural season began on 15 August 1992 with 22 clubs.
  • In 1995, the number of teams was reduced to 20 to improve fixture quality and international competitiveness.
  • The league became a global phenomenon, with massive TV deals (e.g., BSkyB) and international broadcasting in over 200 countries.
  • Clubs like Manchester United, Arsenal, Chelsea, Manchester City, and Liverpool have dominated different eras.
